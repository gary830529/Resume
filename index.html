About me

Hsiang-Yang (Gary) Shyu is a Ph.D. candidate in the Membrane Biotechnology Group in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of South Florida. He is currently conducting research on membrane-based separation technology, advanced adsorbents, and data analysis for the development of an off-grid, autonomous decentralized wastewater treatment system called NEWgenerator™. As part of his Ph.D. research, he analyzes data from a field test system in South Africa, in order to develop dynamic models and focus on optimizing water, nutrient, and energy cycles within the system. Prior to pursuing his Ph.D., Gary obtained his M.S. in Environmental Engineering from USF, during which he conducted research on a flotation microalgae bioreactor for application in aquaponic systems and wastewater treatment plants.
